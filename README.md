## Project Overview

Football clubs, analysts, and scouts rely heavily on player performance data to make informed decisions about team selection, transfers, and tactical planning. This project performs an in-depth Exploratory Data Analysis on a FIFA World Cup 2026 player performance dataset to uncover insights about player characteristics, match performance, and team dynamics.

Through statistical analysis and data visualization, the notebook identifies performance trends, compares players across positions and teams, and highlights the key factors behind on-field success.

---

## Business Problem

Football organizations generate massive volumes of player and match data every tournament, but turning that raw data into meaningful insight is often challenging. This project analyzes the dataset to answer questions such as:

- Which players consistently deliver outstanding performances?
- Which positions contribute the most offensively and defensively?
- How do player attributes influence overall ratings?
- Which teams demonstrate the strongest overall performance?
- What patterns can help clubs improve recruitment and tactical decisions?

The resulting insights are intended to support coaches, analysts, recruiters, and management in making more data-driven decisions.

## Dataset

- **Source:** [FIFA World Cup 2026 Player Performance Dataset](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/rauffauzanrambe/fifa-world-cup-2026-player-performance-dataset) (Kaggle)
- **Size:** 54,600 rows × 75 columns
- **Coverage:** Player information, team information, match information, physical attributes, offensive statistics, defensive statistics, passing statistics, goalkeeping statistics, player ratings, and tournament details

## Key Insights

- The dataset is complete, with no missing values or duplicate records.
- Player ages are concentrated within a relatively narrow range, reflecting a balance of youth and experience.
- Right-footed players significantly outnumber left-footed players.
- Goal scoring is concentrated among a relatively small group of players.
- Creative players consistently contribute through assists and key passes in addition to scoring.
- High passing accuracy alone doesn't necessarily translate into more goal-scoring opportunities.
- Defensive contribution varies considerably across playing positions.
- Performance scores differ by position, reflecting distinct tactical responsibilities.
- Teams with higher creativity scores generally show stronger attacking output.
- Correlation analysis highlights strong relationships among several attacking and defensive metrics.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Evaluate players using multiple performance metrics instead of focusing solely on goals or assists.
- Combine offensive, defensive, and creativity scores when identifying transfer targets.
- Use passing and creativity metrics to spot playmakers who may be undervalued by traditional stats.
- Monitor players with consistently high performance scores across matches for greater reliability.
- Tailor player evaluation criteria to positional responsibilities rather than a one-size-fits-all approach.
- Use defensive contribution metrics to recognize impact that isn't reflected in attacking stats.
- Integrate advanced performance indicators into scouting reports for evidence-based recruitment.

---

## Conclusion

This project performed a comprehensive EDA on a FIFA World Cup player performance dataset containing more than 54,000 player-match records and 75 performance-related features. The analysis explored player demographics, match characteristics, offensive performance, passing quality, defensive actions, overall performance metrics, and team-level comparisons, with correlation analysis highlighting key relationships among numerical variables.

The findings show that player evaluation is most effective when offensive output, defensive contribution, creativity, and physical performance are considered together — illustrating how data analytics can support modern football through objective, data-driven coaching, tactical planning, scouting, and recruitment decisions.
